## Where We Are

Grain is at $3.7M ARR, cash-flow positive, and growing. Customers who activate love us - our NRR is consistently above 100% and domain account retention is trending up. But we have a clear bottleneck: **we don't deliver enough differentiated value when recording, transcription, and AI notes are being commoditized by platforms like Google Meet + Gemini, Claude, and others.** We need to be the obvious "best" solution for someone - not a general notetaker competing against free, bundled alternatives.

## What We Believe About the Future

Recording, transcribing, and taking AI notes is commoditized table stakes bundled into Zoom, Google Meet, and increasingly the major AI platforms themselves. The era of meeting notes as the product is ending.

**What matters now is the data - with a bias for text.** Video is the legacy source of truth for many meetings and we will continue to capture it, but it's not where we plan to invest as a differentiator. Text - transcripts - is what AI tools consume, what enrichment builds on, and what flows into agents. Three things matter:

1. **Universal, reliable, automatic capture.** You can't do anything with meeting data you don't have. Owning capture across every platform (Zoom, Meet, Teams, phone calls) without bots is the foundation. Desktop-based local capture is how we maintain independence from any single conferencing or AI platform.
2. **Enriched transcript data, frictionless to get into AI tools.** Raw transcripts are a commodity. Enriched, contextualized transcripts with speaker & company information, relationship history, live reference links to past meetings, and private notes are not (yet). And they need to flow effortlessly into Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and whatever comes next. The transcript is the new API: AI tools consume it to do real work. ([Meeting Transcript Format Spec](https://www.notion.so/Grain-Meeting-Transcript-Format-Spec-3146689b327f80d1ad7ad4dc287cc476?pvs=21))
3. **Context that builds across projects and relationships.** A single meeting transcript is useful. A project space that accumulates context across every meeting - whether it's a client engagement, an internal initiative, or a cross-functional workstream - with aggregated action items, decision history, and a shared source of truth is transformational. This is where meeting intelligence becomes the start of a system of record, not a one-off artifact.

## Who We Serve

**The AI-Forward, Conversation-Driven Professional.**

This is someone whose primary work product flows from meetings. Not "has meetings" - meetings ARE the work. The meeting is the client delivery (consultant), the strategy session (founder), the discovery call (PM), the deal (AE), the diligence conversation (VC).

This persona shows up as:

- Independent consultants, coaches, and advisors
- Founders
- Some CSMs, AEs, and PMs
- Some VCs

What unifies them isn't their role. It's that:

- They have **ongoing relationships** where context compounds across meetings - not one-off calls, but recurring conversations with clients, prospects, partners, and teammates over weeks and months
- Their **action items, deliverables, and decisions** all originate in conversations
- They need that context **organized by project or relationship** - whether that's a client engagement, an internal initiative, or a cross-functional workstream - not dumped in a single timeline
- They increasingly want to **feed that context into AI tools** to do work - writing follow-ups, creating briefs & memos, prepping for calls, tracking what's open, generating deliverables - and connect it to their other tools: Slack, Notion, Linear, email, and whatever else they work in

## Why This ICP - And Why Not Sales Managers

This is a deliberate shift from the sales manager ICP we committed to in late 2023. Sales managers offered strong NRR (retain and expand), but it has not led to a fundamentally different business for three reasons we've learned:

1. **Sales managers ultimately want vertical SaaS.** They want Gong, Clari, Salesloft - purpose-built sales platforms that own the full workflow.
2. **There's no path to becoming their system of record.** Their system of record is Salesforce or HubSpot. Grain will always be "middleware."
3. **The market is extremely crowded and PLG hasn't worked effieciently.** Vertical sales SaaS is saturated with well-funded competitors. We never found a PLG breakout in that segment, and outbound sales is not in our DNA.

Meanwhile, the AI-forward, conversation-driven professional is where the market is going. These users are already pulling transcripts into Claude and ChatGPT to do real work. They don't have a system of record for their client relationships - Grain can become that. And they adopt through self-serve, which matches how we build and sell.

**Commitment:** We will hold this ICP through at least Q1 2027.

**Kill criteria:** By Q1 2027, we need to see the makings of a step function - not incremental improvement, but evidence of a fundamentally different trajectory. Specifically:

1. **$500K MRR** with sustained 5%+ monthly growth (currently $329K, growing 2-4% monthly)
2. **15,000+ WAU** (currently 10,810)
3. **1,500+ new signups per week** with week-1 activation above 35% (currently ~1,000/week, 29% activation)
4. **Logo retention at or above 80% annually** (industry benchmark for SMB SaaS)

If we're not there or clearly trending toward it, we revisit the ICP. The goal is evidence that this ICP and strategy are producing a different kind of business than what the board has seen for the past 6 years.

## Their Needs

**The Accountability Gap.** Action items get made in meetings and then lost. They need aggregated action items across meetings, organized by client and project, with AI's help to track and execute.

**The Admin Tax.** Hours spent reconstructing context that should just be there - reviewing past notes before calls, formatting summaries after, manually moving transcripts into AI tools. The real tax is maintaining context across meetings.

**Multi-Client and Project Organization.** Managing many active relationships and initiatives with no clean separation - whether that's eight client engagements, internal projects, or both. They need project spaces that accumulate context, aggregate action items, and allow selective sharing without exposing unrelated work.

**Getting Context Into AI.** They want to feed meeting intelligence - scoped by project, person, or company - into Claude, ChatGPT, and other AI tools to do real work. Bulk transcripts, enriched context, one-click handoff.

## Our Strategy

**Grain organizes your meeting intelligence by relationship and project - so every conversation builds on the last, action items never fall through the cracks, and your AI tools have the full context to do the work.**

**The foundation is owning universal capture through our desktop app.** Local, desktop-based capture is what makes everything else possible. It's how we record every meeting regardless of platform, how we capture without intrusive bots, and how we maintain independence from any single conferencing or AI platform. Without our own desktop capture, we're dependent on integrations that can be shut off or replicated. With it, we control the data source.

**Our defensibility (what we're building toward - not what we have today; these need further stress-testing):**

1. **Portability.** Grain captures for every AI tool - not locked into Claude, Google, or any single platform. This is a real differentiator today, but it has a window: Claude and ChatGPT are both building memory and context features. We need to build enough value on top of portability before the platforms close the gap.
2. **Compounding context.** Six months of project and relationship history is dramatically more valuable than any single transcript - and a platform just starting to capture has none of it. This only works if users retain long enough for context to accumulate, which means solving onboarding and activation first.
3. **Purpose-built for meeting-driven work.** AI platforms won't build project spaces, client organization, cross-meeting action items, or client-facing sharing - meetings are a feature for them, not the product. This is our strongest structural argument, but it requires Projects to ship and gain adoption.
4. **Multi-stakeholder lock-in.** When clients, partners, and teammates depend on Grain-based project context, switching disrupts relationships beyond just your own workflow. This is the most durable moat but the furthest out - it requires Projects with client sharing to be live and adopted.

**The long-term trajectory: Grain becomes the CRM that builds itself from your conversations.** Every meeting automatically populates contact records, relationship history, action items, and decision logs. As a consultant or founder grows their practice, Grain grows with them - from meeting capture to project intelligence to a complete system of record for client relationships. No manual data entry. No separate CRM. Your calls build your CRM.

**CRM gate:** We don't want projects or other efforts to to include CRM features (contact records, company pages, relationship timelines) until we explicity go all-in on this. They only make sense if users are already treating Grain as their system of record for client relationships. Before any CRM investment enters the roadmap, Projects v1 needs to show clear evidence of this:

1. **Active project creation** - a meaningful % of active users have created at least one project with auto-population rules (organizing by client/project, not just using Grain as a meeting feed)
2. **Repeat engagement** - users are returning to project views, not just creating and forgetting them (accumulated context is actually useful)
3. **AI export usage** - users are loading project context into AI tools (the "context into AI" thesis is working at the project level, not just individual meetings)

If users aren't organizing their client and customer work around projects in Grain, adding CRM features won't fix it.

## What We're Building

### 1. Owned, reliable, universal capture

[Product Brief: Owned, universal, reliable capture](https://www.notion.so/Product-Brief-Owned-universal-reliable-capture-31b6689b327f80efb1b3d5d6ef74be8c?pvs=21)

Building Grain-owned audio and video capture to replace third-party SDKs. Desktop-based local capture is the foundation - it's how we record every meeting regardless of platform, capture without intrusive bots, and maintain independence from any single conferencing or AI platform.

Voice IDs and LLM-powered speaker identification are a core primitive in this workstream, not a nice-to-have. Identity - knowing who said what - is what makes enrichment, projects, and relationship context possible. Without reliable speaker identification, every layer above capture is weaker.

### 2. Enriching transcripts and getting them into AI tools

[Product Brief: Enriched AI Transcript - Download, Save, and Open Transcript in AI](https://www.notion.so/Product-Brief-Enriched-AI-Transcript-Download-Save-and-Open-Transcript-in-AI-31a6689b327f80ad8830f747bbe8a579?pvs=21)

Implementing the [Meeting Transcript Format Spec](https://www.notion.so/Grain-Meeting-Transcript-Format-Spec-3146689b327f80d1ad7ad4dc287cc476?pvs=21) and building zero-friction paths to get enriched transcripts into every AI tool: Markdown download, "Open in Claude/ChatGPT" deep links, bulk copy across meetings/clips/Stories/Playlists, enriched format via MCP, and local folder sync from the desktop app. Raw transcripts are a commodity - enriched transcripts with relationship context, speaker info, and live reference links are not. And they need to flow effortlessly into Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and whatever comes next.

Committed scope is chunks 1-5 of the brief (enrich transcript format, render as Markdown, downloadable .md, temporary URL for "Open in Claude/ChatGPT," and expose enriched format via MCP). Chunk 6 (local folder sync from the desktop app) earns its way in based on adoption of 1-5.

### 3. Projects

[Product Brief: Projects](https://www.notion.so/Product-Brief-Projects-31b6689b327f80f789fee0fa7db427c1?pvs=21)

Building project spaces where meetings auto-populate based on rules (domains, emails, titles), context accumulates across meetings, and the whole project can be loaded into AI tools for deep work. Projects work for both external engagements (client work, partnerships) and internal initiatives (product workstreams, planning cycles). The design principle: projects should feel auto-generated and context-aware, not like another system to manage. Users shouldn't need to do data entry in Grain to get the benefit - meetings organize themselves based on who's in the room and what's being discussed.

## How We're Sequencing It

**The critical path is: capture → enrich → deliver data into AI tools.** That's the complete value chain. A user records a meeting, Grain enriches the transcript, and it flows into their AI tools to do real work. Everything else - projects, CRM, advanced features - layers on top of this pipeline. Enriched transcripts are the fastest path to differentiation, the lowest engineering cost, and the most measurable. Prerequisite capture work is already in flight and will complete this quarter. Capture is front-end heavy and transcript enrichment is backend heavy, so they run in parallel with different engineers without competing for resources.

1. **Land prerequisite capture + ship enriched transcript chunks 1-3 (this quarter).** Download app and onboard users onto local desktop capture as the primary method that auto-shares with teams & with bots as an option later in the journey. Test local video capture validity using the Recall SDK. In parallel, ship enriched transcript format, Markdown download, and "Open in Claude/ChatGPT." These are targeted for completion this quarter. After prerequisite capture ships, that workstream takes a back seat.

2. **Nail transcript enrichment (next dedicated focus).** Complete the remaining enrichment work: bulk copy, MCP improvements, and proving that enriched transcripts flowing into AI tools is a real differentiator. This is the must-nail work before later investments and the next priority to strengthen the aha. If users love it, we have signal. If they don't, we've learned cheaply.

3. **Projects v1.** A simple v1 replacing playlists: auto-rules to add meetings to a project, MCP support for querying project context, and the ability to load an entire project's context into AI tools. The more purpose-built features (aggregated action items, client sharing, project updates, "AI do this") earn their way in based on v1 adoption.

4. **Replace Recall SDK for transcript capture.** Return to owned capture and replace the Recall SDK on Mac. Must-do for cost, quality, and independence. Local desktop-based video capture is conditional; it earns its way in based on user signal.

## How We Grow

Two primary loops:

1. **Word of mouth.** If we nail the product for this ICP - enriched transcripts that flow into AI tools, projects that organize client context, capture that just works - these users tell other consultants, founders, and professionals in their network. This is a persona that shares workflows & talks about AI and tools that help them leverage it.

2. **SEO targeting AI-native workflows.** We're shifting SEO away from "Conversation Intelligence" and toward the terms this ICP searches for: AI Meeting Agent, AI Notetaker, AI Meeting Tools, and specific workflow examples that Grain powers. Content will be built from case studies and our own experience using Grain to drive AI-native workflows.

If the ICP hypothesis is right, these two channels should be enough to hit our growth goals. We'll augment with a Product Hunt launch when there's a strong story to tell and possibly some KOL work, but those are amplifiers - not the core loops.

## Market Developments to Watch

Things that would change our plan if they happen:

1. **OpenAI's app platform (or major Anthropic moves).** If OpenAI or Anthropic build app platforms where third-party tools like Grain become native data sources inside ChatGPT or Claude, it could accelerate our "get transcripts into AI tools" strategy dramatically - or make parts of it unnecessary. This could also change the priority of MCP vs. platform-specific integrations.

2. **Local capture investments from AI platforms.** If Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini start capturing meetings themselves (via desktop apps they already have or are building), the "owned capture" moat erodes. Our response would be to double down on enrichment, projects, and compounding context - the layers above raw capture.

3. **MCP adoption and standardization.** If MCP becomes the universal standard across all AI tools, it changes the priority of local file sync vs. MCP investment. If it stalls or fragments, local files and platform-specific integrations may become far more important.

4. **Business task agent ecosystem.** If agents emerge that can execute business tasks (draft emails, create docs, update CRMs) via API, the "AI do this" feature in Projects may shift from "hand off context to Claude/ChatGPT" to somethinng like "assign action items to specialized agents" - similar to how Linear assigns issues to coding agents today.

5. **AI memory and context features.** Claude and ChatGPT are both building persistent memory. If they get good at remembering past conversations and uploaded files, the "compounding context" defensibility pillar weakens - users might just feed transcripts into their AI tool and let it remember everything. Our response might be to emphasize the structured, project-level organization that memory alone can't replicate.
